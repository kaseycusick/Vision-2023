Onboarding Buddy Play


Use this play to successfully onboard new employees. Onbaording buddies guide new employees through the entire onboarding process.

New Employee Onboarding Checklist

Step 1. Manager assigns you as an onboarding buddy

Your manager will let you know if you are an onboarding buddy. They have a checklist for the new hire and should give you access.

Step 2. Manage the checklist

You ensure all items on the checklist are completed. You'll have tasks on the list to complete. Other people will also have tasks on the list.

This new employee will rely on you as they navigate a new job. To help them, you make sure those tasks on the checklist are completed so they are onboarded successfully.

All the necessary tasks are in the checklist. This play provides context on some key tasks.

Step 3. Before the employee starts

The manager will coordinate with you before sending out the welcome email to the new employee. This email provides:

  • Date/time of their first day
  • Forms for the new employee to fill out

    • When the employee sends back their badge form, you'll need to:

    • Send this form to the Security Baddging office
    • Forms for the new employee to fill out

      • Step 4. The employee's first day

      The most important thing is to make the employee feel welcome and comfortable. Introduce yourself as their onboarding buddy. Encourage them to ask questions today or any time. You are their lifeline.

      What you need to do

      When the employee sends back their badge form, you'll need to:

      Refer to all the tasks on the checklist. Highlights:

      • Meet the employee in person in front of PG1 and provide a tour
      • Connect them with IT Helpdesk to receive their equipment on the checklist
      • Cell phone set-up can be tricky. Here are some tips:
      • First, have them set up a google account using their work email. They will use this account to set up their cell phone.
      • Closely follow the instructions on Configuring Mobile Devices
      • Obtain the employee's badge (if possible with Security).

        • If there is downtime, remind them of reading materials and training.

        Step 5. The employee's first week

        This week, the new employee will get to learn about CDT, OET, and the team. They also get to know the other members on the team. You'll continue to be their main point of contact. As they go through onboarding and work with the team, they will develop relationships and become more comfortable.

        What you need to do

        • Review the checklist
        • Coordinate with your team. Ask them to schedule meetings for their tasks and follow up with them.

          • Additional resources

          • https://portal.cdt.ca.gov/http/icentral.technology.ca.gov/office-relocations/

            • Step 6. In-depth training and support

            We want to guide the new employee along in their role. Start with foundational items and work up to expert level tasks. An example of this is:

            • Team overviews, by discipline
            • Sit-alongs with all team members to help them understand the team
            • Basic and foundational trainings
            • Beginning training and assignments related to their role
            • In-depth training and assignments related to their role

              • What you need to do

              Ask your team to help you fill in this part of the checklist (if not yet completed or need more). Get insight from new members on ways to make this better.
