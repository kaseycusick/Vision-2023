OET Playbook

Onboarding is more than equipment and paperwork. It's making new hires feel welcome, helping them learn their role, and providing them support to be successful.

New Employee Onboarding Checklist

Use these plays to sucessfully onboard new employees. It covers the period between accepting a job offer, when an employee knows their role, and how to do their job with ease.

Hiring Manager Play

Responsible for initiating onboarding and finding an onboarding buddy.

Onboarding Buddy Play

Responsible for guiding the new employee through the onboarding process.

New Hire Play

Essential for the successful onboarding of a new employee.

Roles and responsibilities

We all play a part in helping our new employees feel welcome and supported.

  • Hiring manager: Responsible for initating onboarding and finding an onboarding buddy.
  • Onboarding buddy: Essential for the successful onboarding of a new employee. Owns the onboarding checklist and makes sure everything is completed. Main point person for the new hire. Typically a peer on the same team.
  • Team: As a peer, you'll play a huge part in the success of helping the new hire learn their roles and how to work.
  • Leadership: Go out of your way to introduce yourself, your team, and what you do.

    • Before you start onboarding

    To successfully onboard employees, you'll ned to tailor the checklist for your team. Either you and/or your team need to determine what:

  • Team overviews should be on the checklist (role specific)
  • Team specific tool training (general)
  • Trainings for specific roles (discplines)
